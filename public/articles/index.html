ADHD Resources & Guides

Evidence-informed articles for ADHD brains at work and at home.

FocusBro is more than a set of tools — it's a companion for ADHD adults navigating a world that wasn't designed with their brains in mind. This resource section brings together evidence-informed articles, practical guides, and deep dives into the strategies that actually work for ADHD. Not generic productivity fluff, not motivational platitudes, but specific techniques grounded in ADHD neuroscience and the lived experience of people who manage ADHD every day.

Every article in this section is written with one audience in mind: adults with ADHD who are smart enough to know when advice is too vague to be useful, experienced enough to be skeptical of miracle solutions, and determined enough to keep searching for what actually works. We draw on research from ADHD specialists including Dr. Russell Barkley, Dr. Ned Hallowell, Dr. Thomas Brown, and peer-reviewed journals including the Journal of Attention Disorders, the Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry, and Neuropsychology Review.

The tools described in these guides — Pomodoro timers, breathing exercises, grounding techniques, binaural beats, ambient sounds, energy trackers — are all available for free in FocusBro. The goal of this section is to explain why these tools work, not just how to use them, so you can build a system that makes sense for your particular brain rather than just following instructions you don't believe in.

More articles are added regularly. If there's a specific ADHD challenge you'd like covered — rejection sensitive dysphoria, ADHD and sleep, working memory strategies, ADHD in remote work, hyperfocus and burnout cycles — send your suggestion to support@focusbro.net.
