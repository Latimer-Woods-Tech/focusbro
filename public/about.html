About FocusBro

Built for ADHD brains, by people who get it.

FocusBro is a free, browser-based ADHD productivity toolkit designed to help people with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (and really, anyone who struggles with focus, time management, or executive dysfunction) do their best work without burning out. Every tool in FocusBro was selected based on research, lived experience with ADHD, and one simple principle: your brain works differently, and your tools should too.

There's no account required. There's no subscription. Your data lives in your browser — private by default. FocusBro is a single page you open in a browser tab, and everything is immediately available: no loading screens, no onboarding friction, no clutter.

Why We Built FocusBro

Working with ADHD in a modern office or remote environment is genuinely hard in ways that neurotypical productivity advice completely misses. Standard guidance like "just make a to-do list" or "block your calendar" ignores the core challenges that ADHD brains face every day:

  • Time-blindness: ADHD affects how the brain perceives the passage of time. A meeting in 20 minutes feels the same as one in 3 hours — until it's 2 minutes away. Countdown timers and visible clocks aren't optional extras; they're essential scaffolding.
  • Hyperfocus: The flip side of distraction is the inability to stop once you're in a flow state. Without an external interruption (a timer, a reminder), hyperfocus can absorb entire days, leaving other important work untouched and your body exhausted.
  • Executive dysfunction: Starting tasks is often harder than completing them. "Just start" advice ignores that for ADHD brains, initiation requires far more mental energy than it does for neurotypical people. FocusBro reduces start-up friction by making the next action immediately obvious.
  • Emotional dysregulation: ADHD frequently co-occurs with anxiety and intense emotional responses. The breathing and grounding tools in FocusBro aren't wellness add-ons — they're direct supports for the nervous system dysregulation that many ADHD people experience, especially before or after difficult tasks.
  • Sensory overwhelm: Open-plan offices, household noise, and unpredictable environments are kryptonite for many ADHD brains. Ambient sound tools help create a consistent audio environment that reduces the cognitive load of filtering out unexpected sounds.

FocusBro was built to address each of these challenges with a specific, evidence-informed tool — not a generic productivity philosophy.

The Science Behind the Tools

Each core tool in FocusBro is backed by a research base worth understanding:

  • Pomodoro Technique: Developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s, the Pomodoro Technique uses timed work intervals (typically 25 minutes) separated by short breaks. Research on time-boxing and forced context switching shows these intervals are particularly effective for ADHD because they create artificial urgency, break tasks into cognitively manageable chunks, and deliver predictable reward checkpoints — all of which support dopaminergic motivation pathways that are underactive in ADHD brains.
  • Breathing Exercises: Slow, controlled breathing directly stimulates the vagus nerve, activating the parasympathetic nervous system and reducing cortisol and adrenaline levels. Studies published in the Journal of Neurological Sciences and elsewhere have documented measurable reductions in anxiety and improvements in working memory following brief respiratory interventions. For ADHD brains that are often in a state of low-grade hyperarousal, this regulation is directly relevant to focus capacity.
  • 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding: This sensory grounding technique, widely used in trauma-informed cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and mindfulness-based cognitive therapy (MBCT), works by redirecting the brain's attention from internal rumination to immediate sensory input. For ADHD, which involves dysregulation of the default mode network (DMN), engaging the senses creates a reliable off-ramp from thought spiraling and re-engages executive attention.
  • Binaural Beats: When two tones of slightly different frequencies are presented separately to each ear, the brain perceives a third "beat" at the frequency difference. EEG research has documented that binaural beats in the alpha range (8–14 Hz) are associated with relaxed alertness, while beta range (14–30 Hz) frequencies correlate with active concentration. Several peer-reviewed studies have found meaningful improvements in attention and working memory in subjects who listened to binaural beats during cognitive tasks.

Privacy-First by Design

We take privacy seriously, especially given that FocusBro users may be tracking health-relevant information like medication schedules, energy levels, and focus difficulties. Our architecture reflects this:

  • All app data is stored in your browser's localStorage by default — never sent to our servers
  • No account or signup is required to use any feature
  • Cloud sync is optional and opt-in only
  • We display ads via Google AdSense (as disclosed in our Privacy Policy), which may use cookies — but we give you full cookie consent controls
