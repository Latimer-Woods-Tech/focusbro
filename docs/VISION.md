# FocusBro — Vision

> One page. Changes rarely. Founder-voice: build loops may propose edits via PR but never merge thesis changes silently.

## Who it's for
Individuals who want structure for their focus — students, remote workers, and anyone using ADHD-adjacent strategies (externalized timers, task chunking, ambient sound, visible progress) to start and finish work sessions. They arrive needing a tool that works immediately, free, with no account required.

## The problem
Starting and sustaining focused work is hard, and the standard advice ("just concentrate") doesn't help people whose attention needs external scaffolding. Existing Pomodoro tools are either paywalled, ad-cluttered to the point of being distracting, or bare timers with no guidance on *how* to use focus techniques well.

## What winning looks like
An 18-month picture: focusbro.net is a genuinely useful free focus toolkit (Pomodoro timer, tasks, stats, ambient sound) wrapped in an educational guides library that earns organic search traffic on focus/productivity queries. Concretely: AdSense approval won and retained, 16+ guides indexed, meaningful organic sessions/month (four figures), and ad revenue that covers infrastructure with margin. Users return because the tool and the guides are honestly good, not because of engagement tricks.

## Monetization thesis
AdSense display advertising on the content layer. The app itself stays free and uncluttered; the guides library is the ad-monetized surface that also drives the organic acquisition. Money enters when Google approves the site as a publisher (genuine content, compliant policy pages, honest features) and search traffic compounds. No one pays FocusBro directly today — the visitor's attention on useful content is the product.

## Non-goals
- **Paid subscriptions (for now)** — pre-traffic, a paywall would kill the acquisition loop the ad thesis depends on; revisit only after organic traffic is real.
- **Any "presence-faking" features** — the "Keep Teams/Slack green" widget, keystroke-injection companion scripts, and synthetic activity simulation are permanently rejected: they are Google "enabling dishonest behavior" policy violations and were a root cause of the AdSense rejection.
- **Marketing-fluff content** — no testimonial surfaces, no filler articles, no fabricated citations. Guides must be accurate and genuinely useful or they don't ship. Never the word "AI" in user-facing copy.

## Kill-signals
- A second AdSense rejection *after* the 16-guide content layer is live and the ~2–4 week indexing wait has been respected → stop resubmitting; re-evaluate the monetization thesis (affiliate, donation, or park).
- Organic search traffic stays ~0 for 4 months after the guides are indexed → the content-for-traffic thesis is falsified; pause investment and park.
- Guide production requires fabricating research or padding word counts to hit volume → stop; thin-but-honest beats thick-and-fake.

## Portfolio position
Standalone LWT portfolio asset — lowest priority tier (Selfprime → Factory → Capricast → COH → XicoCity come first). It feeds the portfolio a proven AdSense/content-layer playbook if it works; it must never draw engineering time from revenue-primary apps. No sister-app coupling: no shared DB, no shared auth.
